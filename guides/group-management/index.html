Complex Group Types

Household Management

2-8 members

Long-term shared living with recurring expenses, utilities, and household purchases.

Key Features:

  • Recurring expense templates for rent, utilities
  • Proportional splitting based on income or room size
  • Automated monthly settlement reminders
  • Shopping list integration for shared purchases
Best Practice:

Set up automatic monthly settlements and use percentage-based splitting for major expenses like rent.

Large Event Coordination

10-100+ members

Weddings, conferences, large parties with multiple organizers and varying participant levels.

Key Features:

  • Hierarchical organizer roles with different permissions
  • Budget categories with spending limits and alerts
  • Attendee participation levels (full cost vs. partial)
  • Multi-phase expense timeline (planning, execution, cleanup)
Best Practice:

Create subgroups for different event aspects (catering, venue, entertainment) with dedicated organizers.

Business Team Expenses

5-50 members

Corporate teams with expense policies, approval workflows, and integration with accounting systems.

Key Features:

  • Approval workflows for expenses over certain amounts
  • Integration with accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero)
  • Policy enforcement and spending limit alerts
  • Detailed reporting and audit trails
Best Practice:

Set up approval chains and integrate with your existing expense management workflow.

Disputed Expenses

When group members disagree about an expense amount, category, or participants.

Resolution Process:

  1. Member flags expense as disputed with reason
  2. Admin or moderator reviews evidence (receipts, photos)
  3. Group discussion period (3-7 days)
  4. Admin makes final decision or initiates group vote
